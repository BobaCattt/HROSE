How HROSE works.

HROSE — the Hypertension Risk Online Sensory Evaluator — is a prototype research tool that combines neurocognitive performance testing with basic biometric data to build a dataset for cardiovascular risk analysis. The assessment takes approximately five minutes and requires no specialist equipment.

The Four Modules

Module 1 · Cognitive

Reaction Time

A box switches from red to green at a random interval. Click the moment it turns green. Five trials are conducted. Mean reaction time and standard deviation across trials (variability) are both recorded. Elevated variability has been associated with reduced cardiovascular and neural efficiency in research literature.

Module 2 · Cognitive

Decision Speed

Ten numbers are displayed in sequence. Classify each as odd or even as quickly as possible. Accuracy and per-trial response time are captured. Decision speed reflects prefrontal processing efficiency, which can be impacted by hypertensive states and reduced cerebrovascular perfusion.

Module 3 · Cognitive

Working Memory (Adaptive Digit Span)

A sequence of digits is briefly displayed, then hidden. You must enter the sequence from memory. Sequence length starts at 4 digits and increases by one with each correct answer (up to 8). The test ends the first time a wrong answer is given, provided at least three rounds have been completed. Your maximum span reached and overall accuracy are both recorded.

Module 4 · Biometric

Biometric Intake

Age, resting heart rate, body weight, height, and sex are collected. BMI is computed automatically from weight and height.

Data Collection

All responses are submitted directly to a secure research spreadsheet upon completion. No score is shown to participants — data is retained exclusively for researcher analysis. The site visit counter is tracked server-side and is consistent across all devices.

This tool is intended for educational and research exploration only. It does not produce a clinical diagnosis. Do not use HROSE as a substitute for professional medical evaluation or clinical cardiovascular risk assessment.
